Background:
Featuring 1,600 houses across 25 neighborhoods throughout Ames in Iowa, the Ames Housing dataset focuses on 79 explanatory variables for each house. In particular, this dataset revolves around specific details on these houses such as their overall condition or type of roof.

Research Questions:
For our project, we will be analyzing and using this dataset to answer four research questions:

  1. "How does a house's lot size in square feet directly affect its sale price?"
  2. "Does the year during which a house was constructed have a significant impact on its sale price?"
  3. "Is there an association between a house's masonry veneer area in square feet and its sale price?"
  4. "Could there be a connection between a house's ground living area in square feet and its sale price?"

Reasoning:
To begin with, the first question is important to study as its answer would explain how a house's lot area contributes to its total sale price. In other words, this would show whether a house's lot area is a key factor in estimating its price or not. Then, the second question is useful since it would be quite interesting to see if there is a relationship between a house's "age" and its sale price. Furthermore, for homeowners who are planning to sell their house, the result to this question could help them figure out the value of their property. Furthermore, the third question is beneficial because its answer can highlight which masonry veneer sizes are more likely to result in a higher or lower sale price. By looking at the data, the same homeowners would also have a better idea of how well their house would do in the market. Finally, the last question is practical to answer as it can demonstrate how the dimensions of the ground living area contribute to a house's sale price. This in turn would help those homeowners understand which price would be appropriate for their house based on its ground living area.

Part 5 Multiple Linear Regression

  • Our four features (x) that we will be analyzing include the house's lot area, the year during which it was built, the area of its masonry veneer, and its grid living area.
  • Meanwhile, the target (y) that we will focus on is the house's sale price.
  • So, our equation for this type of linear regression will look like: $$Predicted Sale Price = b_0() + b_1(LotArea) + b_2(YearBuilt) + b_3(MasVnrArea) + b_4(GrLivArea),$$ where $$b_0, b_1, b_2, b_3, and b_4$$ are the parameters for this model.
